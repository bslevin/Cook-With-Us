About Us

The chef

Sandra Helena is a Spanish chef and restaurateur. She became a chef in 1997, working at the restaurant Roanne, with Claude Troisgros and Emmanuel Bassoleil. She has interned at the restaurants Sadler, in Italy and El Celler de Can Roca, in Spain. She decided in 2006, with her husband Daniel Redondo and associates, to open the restaurant La Brava. La Brava restaurant won its first Michelin Star in 2011 and second in 2016. Their restaurant has been placed on "The Worlds Best Restaurant List" since 2014. It is a must-visit restaurant for visitors to Bilbao. Most recently, Sandra was a guest chef on the Netflix "Chef Table" series, sitting alongside Europe's top chefs and noticeably belonging at the table.

Our kitchen

Our kitchen was purpose built on Sandra's family estate in Northern Spain. The estate has been in the family since 1940 and here, with her grandmother, Sandra began to learn to cook. Sandra's youth was immersed in agriculture and family meals, a big part of forming who she is today. Apart from the cooking school, her brother runs the wine orchard on the property. They produce a wine called, Txakoli, unique to the Bizikaia region. Also on the property, Sandra has an active green house, where most of the produce used in their restaurant is grown.

Our Courses

Italian

It is the first course beginning in spring, as the green house starts to produce. Sandra believes it is essential to learn the use of herbs and Italian flavours are essential for any person wishing to enter a professional kitchen environment.

Spanish

Sandra's cooking heritage and flair is on full display. You learn and watch her plan menus, whilst organising the ingredients. You meet the farmer of the estate and gain insight into how a chef and their ingredients are crucial to each other's performance.

French

The Basque region runs from Bilbao in Spain, through the Pyrenees, to Bayonne in France. Obviously, why are so many Michelin-star restaurants located in such a small area. The surrounding area is full of hazelnut and walnut trees, undoubtedly finding their way into the cuisine. You learn how Autumn is a different, more exquisite cooking tradition.

What we offer

Three separate courses, each running for 12 weeks. With the aim of involving students in the food traditions journey. You learn the planning of menus, how having alternative menus in mind is art. Our environment is that of a busy kitchen but, with more room to ask and learn. While each day's menu is cooked to be served on the farm to employees and guests. Sandra believes food creates the atmosphere that brings people together.
The course has a capacity of 6 trainee chefs. On the 3rd week, pairs will alternate in the Michelin-Star restaurant.
